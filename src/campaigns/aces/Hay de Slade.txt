Hay de Slade, Henri
France

   Henri Joseph Marie Hay de Slade was born 29 May 1893 in the Atlantic port city of Brest. His father was a Naval Officer of Irish descent, and a member of the aristocracy. He entered the Special Military School at St Cyr in 1913, but gained his release when war broke out and enlisted as a simple soldat with the 20e Chasseurs à Cheval. He was promoted and transferred to the 14e Hussards in March 1915, and then transferred to the Aviation Militaire on 11 May 1916. 
  After advanced training, Hay de Slade was assigned to Esc N80 on 13 December 1916. On 5 December '17, he became the first 'Ace' of SPA 86 with his fifth victory, a Rumpler over Anizy. On 28 July Hay de Slade assumed command of SPA 159, an  Escadrille which had not scored a single victory since it's inception at the start of the year. Under his leadership, it was credited with 12 victories, eight of them his, by EoW. He had flown 1200 hours, held the Croix de Guerre with fourteen palmes, the Belgian Croix de Guerre, and Britain's MC.
  After the war, Comte Henri  Hay de Slade was made a Grand Officier of the Legion d'Honneur. He died on 2 November 1979.
