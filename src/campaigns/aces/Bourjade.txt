Bourjade, Léon
France

   Léon Jean-Pierre Bourjade was born in Montauban on 25 May 1889. From a very early age Bourjade dreamed of a religious life. He became a novitiate in 1908, and was studying theology in Fribourg, Switzerland when the Great War broke out. He nevertheless returned to France and enlisted with the artillery. After the Battle of the Marne he was promoted to Marechal des Logis. During 1915 he requested a transfer to a trench mortar unit, and he was assigned to the 125e Brigade de Bombardiers, where he received a promotion to Sous Lieutenant.  
  Two years later he requested a transfer to the aviation service, and received his Brevet 17 June 1917. After further training,he was assigned on 13 September to Esc N.152 where he remained until War's end.  By the end of the war, he was credited with downing 27 balloons and 1 enemy aircraft and was the highest scoring ace to serve with this unit.  He was made a Chevalier of the Legion d'Honneur on 5 June 1918, and he was also awarded the Croix de Guerre with 17 Palmes and one Etoile de Vermeil.      
  After the war Léon Bourjade resumed his studies in Switzerland and was ordained in 1921. In November of that year he left for Yui Island in British New Guinea, to serve as a missionary. He died there 22 October 1924, having followed his dream.
