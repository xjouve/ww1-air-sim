Heurtaux, Alfred
France

   Alfred Marie Joseph Heurtaux was born 20 May 1893 in Nantes, the son of an artillery officer. After attending Military College, he was commissioned Sous-Lieutenant in the 9ème Hussars. Wounded by an Uhlan's lance in one of the first skirmishes in August 1914, he transferred to the air service as an observer with MS26 on 6 December 1914. 
  Heurtaux was soon enrolled as a student pilot at Pau, earned his brevet 29 May 1915, and was posted to MS38. On 5 June 1916 he reported to Esc N3 - Les Cigognes- and assumed command on 16 June. He was severely wounded 5 May 1917, but returned to resume command 7 August. However, he was severely wounded again 3 September 1917; his war flying was over. He had 21 confirmed victories and thirteen probables.
  In WWII, he was the Inspector of Fighter Aviation, and after the fall of France, served with the Resistance. Arrested by the Gestapo and deported to Buchenwald, he miraculously survived and was liberated in April 1945. In December of that year, Heurtaux was promoted to Général de Brigade Aérienne. Alfred Heurtaux died 30 December 1985 at Chantilly.
